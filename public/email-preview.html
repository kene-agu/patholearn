Clinical History

A 58-year-old male presents with a 3-month history of painless haematuria and a palpable right flank mass. CT abdomen shows a 7 cm heterogeneous renal mass with areas of necrosis. Nephrectomy is performed and the specimen is sent for histological assessment.

Age 58M
Organ Kidney
Stain H&E
Microscopic Findings
Architecture
Nests and acini separated by a delicate sinusoidal vasculature
Cell morphology
Polygonal cells with abundant clear cytoplasm and distinct cell borders
Nucleus
Round, centrally placed; Fuhrman grade 2 nucleoli
Stroma
Thin fibrovascular septa; focal areas of haemorrhage and necrosis
Diagnosis
Clear Cell Renal Cell Carcinoma
WHO/ISUP Grade 2 · pT2a (> 7 cm, confined to kidney)
Immunohistochemistry Profile
CD10 +
Vimentin +
PAX8 +
CA-IX +
CK7 −
TFE3 −
Teaching Points
  • 1
    The clear cytoplasm results from intracellular glycogen and lipid, which dissolve during routine processing — not a true "empty" cell.
  • 2
    VHL gene mutations (chromosome 3p25) are present in ~75% of sporadic cases; VHL inactivation leads to HIF accumulation and VEGF overexpression — the rationale for anti-angiogenic therapy (sunitinib, pazopanib).
  • 3
    CA-IX (carbonic anhydrase IX) is uniformly positive in clear cell RCC and serves as a useful marker in metastatic settings where the primary is unknown.
  • 4
    Distinguish from chromophobe RCC (pale eosinophilic/perinuclear halo, CK7+) and papillary RCC (papillary architecture, AMACR+, foamy macrophages).
