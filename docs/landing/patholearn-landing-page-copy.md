## 4. The problem
**Eyebrow:** The problem
**H2:** Histopathology is learned by seeing, not reading.

Two cards:
1. **Textbooks are static** — Histopathology is pattern recognition across hundreds of real slides — not diagrams and paragraphs on a page.
2. **Microscope time runs out** — Lab sessions are limited and the slides stay in the lab. Most revision happens from memory, long after the scope is packed away.

## 5. How it works
**Eyebrow:** How it works
**H2:** Built for repetition

Three numbered steps (sequence is real — numbering justified):
- **01 Open a slide** — Real histopathology slides with every diagnostic structure marked and described — zoom in and explore at your own pace.
- **02 Test yourself** — Active-recall questions drawn from each slide and topic, so you're retrieving the pattern rather than re-reading it.
- **03 Return at the right time** — Spaced repetition schedules each card for the moment just before you'd forget it. Progress tracked by topic and slide.
